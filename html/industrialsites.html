LABU ESTATE RUBBER INDUSTRIAL SITE


The Labu Estate Rubber Industrial Site in the Temburong district has potential to become one of Brunei’s tourist attractions and complement its role as an educational site for students and researchers

The Labu Estate Rubber Industry Site was launched on August 5, 2010. An ‘open site museum’ concept, has significant historical, economic and cultural values.

The site is evidence of the commercial plantation and agriculture activity that contributed to the country’s economy back in the 1900s

On the site’s economic value, the grassroots in addition to being the first site for Brunei’s early agricultural era, it was also provided employment to locals.

On the cultural and educational value of the site, it provides opportunities for Brunei’s younger generation to see closely the economic activities practised by the country.


PRESERVATION AND CONSERVATION

     

     

The Museums Department received royal consent to gazette an area of nine acres of the site under the Antiquities and Treasure Trove Act 1967 (revised 1984; 1991; 2002) in February 2006 for preservation.



The site is monitored collectively by the Museums Department and Kg Labu Estate Consultative Council.



The site is popular amongst secondary schools and tertiary level students as well as researchers interested to know more about the rubber industrial site.



The Research and Conservation Project for Labu Estate Rubber Industry Site, Phase 1 started on June 12, 2007 The site houses the smokehouse, dry godown (where dried rubber was weighed and packed for export), generator house and storage hall. The exhibition features the rubber processing in Labu Estate and describes the role and function of the buildings present around the vicinity.
